Light potato and leek soup with a **generous addition of peas** for texture and a modest protein increase.  
No cream; body comes from potatoes and controlled blending.  
Designed as a **low-calorie, high-volume soup** to support meal planning and pair well with protein-forward mains.

## Directions

**Soup**
1. Prepare the vegetables: clean and slice leeks, dice potatoes evenly, chop onion.
2. In a large pot over medium heat, melt the butter or heat the oil.
3. Add onion and leeks with a pinch of salt and cook gently until soft and translucent (8â€“10 min). Do not brown.
4. Add potatoes and vegetable stock. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce to a simmer.
5. Simmer uncovered until potatoes are fully tender (18â€“22 min).
6. Blend the soup until smooth using an immersion blender.
7. Add frozen peas directly to the hot soup and simmer until tender and bright (3â€“5 min).
8. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

## Portioning & Storage

**Per portion (ready to eat):**
- ~450 g soup

**Storage**
- Refrigerator: up to 4 days  
- Freezer: up to 2 months

Freeze in individual portions. Reheat gently and stir well.

## Notes

- Peas are added after blending to preserve colour, texture, and sweetness.
- This soup is intentionally light and volume-forward; protein is supportive, not primary.
- Thickness increases as the soup cools; loosen with hot water when reheating.
- Optional yogurt should be added at serving time to avoid instability during storage.
